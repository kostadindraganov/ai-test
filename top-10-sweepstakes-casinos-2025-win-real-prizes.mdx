### What Are Sweepstakes Casinos, Exactly? (And How They Differ)

Sweepstakes casinos are online platforms offering casino-style games that you can play for free, with the possibility of redeeming winnings for real prizes. They work using a dual-currency system. You'll typically encounter:

*   **Gold Coins (GC):** This is your primary play-for-fun currency. You can usually get Gold Coins for free through daily bonuses, promotions, or by purchasing them. Playing with Gold Coins is just for entertainment; any winnings in GC can't be exchanged for real prizes.
*   **Sweeps Coins (SC):** This is the currency that allows you to redeem winnings for real prizes. Sweeps Coins can't be directly purchased. They're often provided as a free bonus when you buy Gold Coin packages, or through free alternative methods of entry (AMOE), such as mail-in requests or social media contests. When you play games using Sweeps Coins and win, those SC winnings can, subject to the terms and conditions, be redeemed for cash prizes or gift cards.

This structure is what distinguishes sweepstakes casinos from traditional online casinos (where you deposit and wager real money) and social casinos (which use virtual currency with no prize redemption). The key legal difference lies in how sweepstakes casinos avoid being classified as illegal lotteries. A lottery usually involves three things: a prize, chance, and consideration (paying to play). Sweepstakes promotions, to be legal, must remove at least one of these elements. This is usually done by eliminating consideration through the "No Purchase Necessary" rule, meaning you must have a way to obtain Sweeps Coins and enter the sweepstakes for free.

### The Legality of Sweepstakes Casinos in the USA (2024 Update)

The legal framework that allows sweepstakes casinos to operate in most US states hinges on their adherence to sweepstakes laws, particularly the "No Purchase Necessary" requirement. Because players can get Sweeps Coins for free, without mandatory purchase, these platforms are generally considered legal promotional sweepstakes instead of gambling sites.

The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) is the primary federal body overseeing sweepstakes legality and consumer rights in the US, ensuring promotions are fair and not deceptive. While sweepstakes law is largely federal, specific state laws can vary, and some states may have restrictions. Therefore, always verify the legality of sweepstakes casino play in your specific location.
